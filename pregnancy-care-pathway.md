# Pregnancy Care Pathway

## 1. When to Use

Use this skill for any maternity or pregnancy-related referral, including:

- Confirmation of pregnancy and first antenatal registration
- Routine antenatal care across all three trimesters
- Delivery planning and facility selection
- High-risk pregnancy requiring specialist or tertiary care
- Postnatal care and newborn review

## 2. Information Gathering

Before making any recommendation, collect the following:

- Current gestational age or trimester (in weeks if known)
- Patient age (flag if >35)
- Parity and obstetric history (previous C-section, preterm birth, miscarriage)
- High-risk flags: gestational diabetes, hypertension, pre-eclampsia in prior
  pregnancy, twin or multiple pregnancy, placenta issues, fetal growth concerns
- Preferred delivery type (vaginal or caesarean) if stated
- Patient location (district, state) and distance from facilities
- Insurance or scheme enrolment (PMJAY/Ayushman Bharat, ESI, private)
- Language and literacy to assess communication needs

## 3. Trimester-Based Guidance

### First Trimester (Weeks 1-12)

- Confirm pregnancy via urine or blood beta-hCG test
- Register for Antenatal Care (ANC) at nearest government PHC or CHC
- A basic primary health centre is sufficient for low-risk patients at this stage
- Order: complete blood count, blood group and Rh typing, urine routine, VDRL,
  HIV and HBsAg screening, TSH, blood glucose
- Schedule dating scan (ideally before 12 weeks)
- Counsel on folic acid supplementation (5 mg/day until 12 weeks) and iron
- Advise enrolment in JSY and PMMVY if not already done

### Second Trimester (Weeks 13-26)

- Schedule TIFFA anomaly scan between 18 and 20 weeks — confirm the referring
  facility has ultrasound with a trained sonologist; escalate if not available
- Screen for gestational diabetes (OGTT) between 24 and 28 weeks
- Continue ANC at district hospital or private maternity clinic with ultrasound
- A basic hospital with ultrasound capability is sufficient for low-risk cases
- Monitor blood pressure at every visit; flag elevation early

### Third Trimester (Weeks 27-40)

- Shift care to a facility with a functional labour room and operating theatre
  for emergency caesarean section
- Confirm blood bank availability on site or within 30 minutes
- For high-risk pregnancies: facility must have NICU (level 2 or level 3) and
  an adult ICU or HDU
- Delivery planning discussion: birth preparedness, transport plan, companion
- Verify JSY enrolment for cash incentive on institutional delivery
- Postnatal: ensure facility supports 48-hour post-delivery observation, newborn
  screening, and breastfeeding support

## 4. High-Risk Flags

Escalate to a tertiary care centre or teaching hospital if any of the following
are present:

- Pre-eclampsia or eclampsia (current or prior pregnancy)
- Placenta previa or placenta accreta
- Fetal growth restriction (FGR) detected on scan
- Twin, triplet, or higher-order multiple pregnancy
- Gestational diabetes requiring insulin
- Prior uterine surgery (myomectomy, classical C-section)
- Preterm labour before 34 weeks
- Rh-negative mother with sensitisation

## 5. Facility Requirements by Risk Level

| Risk Level | Minimum Facility Requirement |
|------------|------------------------------|
| Low-risk   | District hospital or private maternity home with OT and blood bank access |
| High-risk  | Hospital with NICU level 2+, 24-hour anaesthesia, blood bank on site, ICU |
| Preterm <34 weeks | NICU level 3 mandatory; refer to teaching hospital or SNCU centre |
| Postnatal complications | ICU-capable facility; do not retain at PHC/CHC |

## 6. Government Schemes

Inform patients of applicable central and state schemes:

- Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY): cash incentive for institutional delivery,
  higher amounts for rural BPL beneficiaries and SC/ST women
- Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY): Rs 5,000 maternity benefit for
  first live birth, paid in instalments on ANC milestones
- Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY: covers delivery and complications at empanelled
  hospitals up to Rs 5 lakh per family per year
- State schemes: Mamata Scheme (West Bengal), Aasra (Rajasthan), Sumangala
  (Uttar Pradesh) — check state-specific eligibility at registration

## 7. Guardrails

- Never recommend or endorse home delivery under any circumstances
- Always verify NICU availability before referring a preterm or high-risk
  patient to any facility; do not assume availability from facility name alone
- Never dismiss high-risk flags such as hypertension, bleeding, or reduced
  fetal movements — escalate and document
- Do not delay referral pending insurance authorisation if patient is in distress
- Confirm the patient has a transport plan and a support person for delivery

## 8. Verify

- [ ] Gestational age and trimester confirmed before making facility recommendation
- [ ] High-risk flags screened and documented
- [ ] Recommended facility has appropriate OT, blood bank, and NICU level
      matched to patient risk
- [ ] Government scheme enrolment (JSY, PMMVY) advised or confirmed
- [ ] Patient has a birth preparedness plan including transport and companion
- [ ] Follow-up ANC appointment or postnatal visit date communicated

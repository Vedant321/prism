# Mental Health Referral

## 1. When to Use

Use this skill for any patient presenting with a mental health concern, including:

- Depression, persistent low mood, or loss of interest
- Anxiety, panic attacks, or phobias
- Substance use or addiction (alcohol, opioids, sedatives, other)
- Psychosis, hallucinations, or delusional thinking
- Crisis situations including suicidal ideation or self-harm
- Grief, bereavement, or adjustment difficulties
- Eating disorders (restrictive eating, binge-purge patterns)
- Workplace stress, burnout, or relationship distress

## 2. Crisis vs Non-Crisis Triage

This is the first step — complete before any other assessment or referral.

### Crisis Signals — Escalate Immediately

If any of the following are present, treat as a psychiatric emergency:

- Suicidal ideation with or without a plan, or active self-harm
- Active psychosis: hallucinations (auditory, visual), disorganised speech,
  severe delusions unresponsive to redirection
- Not eating or sleeping for three or more consecutive days
- Aggression or behaviour posing danger to the patient or others

Action: refer immediately to emergency psychiatry department or nearest
hospital casualty. Provide the following helplines while arranging care:

- Vandrevala Foundation: 1860-2662-345 (24x7, multilingual)
- iCall TISS: 9152987821 (Monday to Saturday, 8 am to 10 pm)

### Non-Crisis Presentation

Depression, anxiety, grief, mild-moderate stress, relationship difficulties,
addiction counselling needs — route to outpatient psychiatrist, psychologist,
or community mental health services as appropriate.

## 3. Information Gathering

Collect the following before recommending a level of care:

- Primary presenting concern in the patient's own words
- Duration: how long have symptoms been present
- Crisis signals: active suicidal ideation, self-harm, inability to function
- Location (district, state) to identify accessible services
- Previous psychiatric treatment: hospitalisation, medication, therapy history
- Patient preference: psychiatrist, psychologist, or counsellor
- Language preference — many services are available in regional languages
- Support system: family or caregiver involvement, living situation

## 4. Level of Care Matching

### Outpatient Psychiatrist

Indicated for: medication management of depression, bipolar disorder, OCD,
stabilised schizophrenia, ADHD in adults. Available at district hospitals,
private clinics, and NMHP centres.

### Psychologist or Counsellor

Indicated for: structured therapy (CBT, DBT, supportive counselling) for
anxiety, grief, stress, mild-to-moderate depression, relationship issues.
Available at tertiary hospitals, private practices, NGO centres, iCall.

### Inpatient Psychiatric Unit

Indicated for: active psychosis requiring observation, severe depression with
suicidality, acute mania, medically supervised detox for substance dependence.
Requires admission — check bed availability and patient consent per MHA 2017.

### Community Mental Health

Indicated for: follow-up of stabilised patients, medication adherence support,
psychosocial rehabilitation. Available through NMHP district mental health
clinics; ASHAs and ANMs can support follow-up in rural areas.

## 5. Key Resources

- NIMHANS Bangalore: national reference centre for complex cases, training,
  and research; outpatient and inpatient services
- LGB Regional Institute of Mental Health, Guwahati: primary referral for
  northeast India
- iCall TISS Mumbai: free telephone and online counselling; 9152987821
- Vandrevala Foundation: 24x7 crisis helpline; 1860-2662-345
- SNEHI: NGO providing counselling and support for families; based in Delhi
- The MINDS Foundation: community mental health in rural Maharashtra
- State mental health hospitals: located in each state capital (e.g., Institute
  of Mental Health Chennai, Ranchi Institute of Neuropsychiatry, IHBAS Delhi)
- NMHP district mental health programme clinics: present in most districts;
  free outpatient psychiatric care and medication

## 6. Patient Rights

Under the Mental Healthcare Act 2017, patients have the following rights:

- Right to access mental health treatment without discrimination
- Right to confidentiality — information shared cannot be disclosed without
  consent except in narrowly defined safety situations
- Right to refuse electro-convulsive therapy (ECT) without written informed
  consent; ECT cannot be administered without consent in non-emergency settings
- Right to make an advance directive specifying treatment preferences
- Right to a nominated representative of their choice
- Involuntary admission requires assessment by a mental health professional and
  a board review; it cannot be initiated by family alone

Communicate these rights clearly and in a language the patient understands.

## 7. Guardrails

- Never use stigmatising language: do not say "crazy", "mad", "pagal",
  "mental", or "disturbed" — use "mental health condition" or the clinical term
- Never dismiss crisis signals as attention-seeking or exaggeration — always
  take suicidal ideation seriously and escalate
- Never recommend spiritual healing, religious intervention, or traditional
  practices as a substitute for clinical assessment and care
- Surface crisis helplines (Vandrevala, iCall) on every response where the
  patient expresses distress, even if the presentation does not meet full
  crisis criteria
- Never disclose patient-shared information to family without explicit patient
  consent unless there is an immediate safety risk
- Do not advise patients to stop psychiatric medication on their own — always
  direct to the prescribing psychiatrist for any medication changes

## 8. Verify

- [ ] Crisis triage completed as the first step before any referral
- [ ] Crisis helplines (Vandrevala 1860-2662-345, iCall 9152987821) surfaced
      if any distress is present
- [ ] Level of care matched to presenting severity (outpatient vs inpatient
      vs community)
- [ ] Patient rights under Mental Healthcare Act 2017 communicated where
      relevant (especially for any inpatient referral)
- [ ] Stigma-sensitive language used throughout the response
- [ ] Local or nearest accessible facility identified by patient location
- [ ] Follow-up plan or next contact point confirmed with patient

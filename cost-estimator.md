# Cost Estimator

## 1. When to Use

Trigger this skill when a patient or caregiver is asking about:

- The approximate cost of a medical procedure before deciding where to go
- Whether they can afford a treatment at a private hospital versus a government hospital
- Budget planning for an upcoming surgery or long-term treatment such as dialysis or chemotherapy
- Understanding what out-of-pocket expenses remain after insurance or scheme coverage

## 2. Information Gathering

Before generating an estimate, collect the following:

- **Procedure name**: Ask the patient to confirm the exact procedure name as told by the doctor
- **State and city tier**: The state of treatment and whether the city is Tier-1 (Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata), Tier-2, or Tier-3
- **Hospital type preference**: Government/public hospital or private hospital
- **Insurance or scheme status**: Any active health insurance policy, or whether enrolled under PMJAY, CGHS, ESIS, or a state scheme
- **BPL card status**: Whether the patient holds a Below Poverty Line ration card or is listed in SECC 2011 data
- **Number of procedures planned**: Especially for recurring treatments such as dialysis or chemotherapy cycles

## 3. Procedure Cost Ranges

All figures below are estimates only — call the hospital billing department for actual costs. Costs vary by surgeon, anaesthesia, room category, and implant choice.

**Dialysis (per session)**
- Government hospital: Rs. 0–200 (free for eligible patients under PM National Dialysis Programme)
- Private hospital: Rs. 1,200–2,500

**C-section delivery**
- Government hospital: Rs. 0–2,000
- Private hospital, Tier-1 city: Rs. 60,000–1,50,000
- Private hospital, Tier-2 city: Rs. 35,000–80,000
- Private hospital, Tier-3 city: Rs. 20,000–50,000

**Appendectomy (open)**
- Government hospital: Rs. 500–3,000
- Private hospital: Rs. 30,000–80,000

**Cataract surgery (per eye)**
- Government hospital: Rs. 0–500
- Private hospital: Rs. 15,000–50,000 (range reflects basic to premium intraocular lens)

**Cancer chemotherapy (per cycle)**
- Drug-dependent; typically Rs. 10,000–1,50,000 per cycle
- Government hospital rates are significantly lower and some drugs are available free under national cancer programmes

**Knee replacement (unilateral)**
- Government hospital: Rs. 10,000–30,000
- Private hospital: Rs. 1,50,000–4,00,000

**Angioplasty (single stent)**
- Government hospital: Rs. 15,000–50,000
- Private hospital: Rs. 1,50,000–3,50,000
- Note: stent cost is the largest component; prices are capped under NPPA Drug Price Control Order — ask the hospital for the NPPA ceiling price

## 4. Ayushman Bharat Package Rates

If the patient is eligible for PMJAY (Ayushman Bharat), empanelled hospitals cannot charge more than the PMJAY package rate. Use these as the ceiling to reference:

- **Dialysis**: Covered under the PM National Dialysis Programme at empanelled government hospitals
- **C-section delivery**: Approximately Rs. 9,000 package under PMJAY
- **Knee replacement**: Approximately Rs. 80,000 package under PMJAY
- **Angioplasty**: Approximately Rs. 40,000–60,000 package under PMJAY

Always instruct the patient to verify the current applicable package rate directly at pmjay.gov.in, as rates are periodically revised.

## 5. Cost-Reduction Tips

Share the following with every patient regardless of insurance status:

- Request an itemised bill before discharge; hospitals are legally required to provide one
- For implants such as stents, knee implants, and intraocular lenses, ask the hospital for the NPPA price-capped rate and confirm the implant is not being billed above that ceiling
- Ask the pharmacist or the treating doctor whether a generic equivalent is available under the Jan Aushadhi Yojana or hospital pharmacy
- Check whether the procedure appears on the PMJAY package list even if the patient has not yet enrolled — they may still be eligible
- Ask the hospital's patient welfare officer or social worker about state government scheme entitlements specific to the patient's category (BPL, differently abled, senior citizen)
- For cancer patients, check whether the drug is listed under the National Cancer Grid protocol and available at the government rate

## 6. Guardrails

- Always present costs as a range, never as a single fixed figure
- Clearly state that all figures are estimates; actual costs depend on room type, duration of stay, complications, and implant grade
- Never guarantee a price to the patient or caregiver
- Always recommend calling the hospital billing or insurance desk before admission
- Never quote private hospital costs without first checking whether the patient is eligible for a government scheme — scheme coverage must be explored first
- Do not discourage a patient from seeking care due to cost alone; always surface at least one lower-cost pathway

## 7. Verify

- [ ] Procedure name confirmed with the patient as told by their doctor
- [ ] State, city tier, and hospital type captured
- [ ] BPL card and scheme eligibility checked before quoting private costs
- [ ] Cost presented as a range with the "estimate only" disclaimer included
- [ ] PMJAY package rate cited where procedure is on the PMJAY list
- [ ] At least one cost-reduction tip shared with the patient
- [ ] Patient directed to contact the hospital billing department for a written estimate

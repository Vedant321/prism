# Blood Bank Finder

## 1. When to Use

Trigger this skill when:
- User says "need blood for surgery," "blood required," "blood donchahiye"
- User mentions a specific blood group in the context of a procedure or emergency
- User asks about blood donation for a patient ("where can we donate blood for my father?")
- Condition implies regular transfusion needs: thalassemia, sickle cell disease, aplastic anemia, hemophilia
- Pre-surgical workup is underway and blood availability is a concern

---

## 2. Confirm the Blood Requirement First

Before searching, collect:
- **Blood group**: ABO type + Rh factor (e.g., B positive, O negative, AB positive)
- **Number of units required**: confirm with treating doctor or anaesthetist
- **Timeline**: how many hours/days before surgery or transfusion?
- **Location**: city and ideally the hospital where the procedure will happen

A surgical team typically requires blood to be cross-matched and available in the blood bank of the operating hospital before proceeding. Clarify whether the family needs to:
1. Arrange donor blood at the same hospital's blood bank, OR
2. Source blood from an external blood bank for transfer, OR
3. Identify volunteer donors who will donate in exchange

---

## 3. Primary Sources to Check

**eRaktKosh — National Blood Bank Portal**
- Website: eraktkosh.in
- Government of India portal listing blood availability across registered blood banks nationwide
- Searchable by blood group, city, and hospital
- Availability data may lag by a few hours — always call the blood bank to confirm

**Hospital's In-House Blood Bank**
- First and most reliable source for surgical cases at that hospital
- Cross-matching (compatibility testing) takes 30–60 minutes for routine cases; longer for rare groups
- Ask to speak with the blood bank technician directly for confirmed availability

**State Blood Transfusion Council (SBTC)**
- Each state has an SBTC that coordinates between blood banks
- Contact details available on the state health department website
- Useful when hospital blood bank has a shortage

**Indian Red Cross Society**
- Operates blood banks in major cities; strong voluntary donor base
- Website: indianredcross.org | Helpline: 1800-180-7000

---

## 4. Rare Blood Groups — Escalated Search

For the following blood groups, standard blood bank search is insufficient. Activate an escalated search.

**O-negative (Universal donor — often in shortage)**
- eRaktKosh priority search
- Sankalp India Foundation (sankalpindia.net) — maintains voluntary rare donor registry
- iDonate Blood (iDonateBlood.com / app) — connects donors and patients

**AB-negative**
- Same channels as O-negative
- Ask hospital if they can retrieve from a reference blood bank in the city

**Bombay Blood Group (H-negative / Oh)**
- Extremely rare — affects approximately 1 in 10,000 people in India; more common in certain communities
- Bombay blood group individuals can only receive blood from other Bombay blood group donors
- **Escalate immediately to**: Rare Donors Programme — IDBI Foundation; National Institute of Immunohaematology (NIIH), Mumbai (Tel: 022-24138518); INSA Rare Donor Registry
- Pre-surgical autologous donation (patient donates their own blood in advance of elective surgery) should be discussed with the surgeon

**Thalassemia patients**
- Regular transfusions required (typically every 3–4 weeks)
- Register with the treating hospital's thalassemia day care center for ongoing blood supply coordination
- Thalassemia Society of India maintains patient registries and blood donation drives

---

## 5. What to Ask at the Blood Bank

When calling a blood bank, the caregiver should ask:

1. "Do you have [blood group] available right now? How many units?"
2. "How long does cross-matching take for this patient?"
3. "Do we need to arrange donor blood in exchange, or is voluntary donor blood available?"
4. "Can we pre-book the blood for surgery on [date]?"
5. "What is the process if the blood is not available at your bank — can you coordinate with another bank?"

---

## 6. Voluntary vs. Replacement Donation

- **Voluntary donation** (preferred): blood from registered voluntary donors; safer and more reliable
- **Replacement donation**: family or friends donate in exchange for units used — common in India but carries risk if donors are coerced or ineligible
- Do not coerce family members into donating; inform them of eligibility requirements (18–65 years, >50 kg, not donated in last 3 months, no recent illness or medications)
- Directed donation (only to a named patient) is generally discouraged except for rare groups — explain this to families who insist

---

## Guardrails

- Never guarantee blood availability — stock changes hour by hour; always direct to call the blood bank directly to confirm before proceeding
- For surgical cases: blood must be arranged and cross-matched before the procedure begins, not after — flag this urgency clearly if the surgery is imminent
- For Bombay blood group, treat as a medical emergency-adjacent situation and escalate without delay
- Do not provide blood bank contact numbers that may be outdated — direct users to eraktkosh.in for the current, verified list
- Home transfusion is not safe and is not endorsed; blood transfusion must be administered in a clinical setting with monitoring

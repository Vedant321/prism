# Ambulance Coordinator

## 1. When to Use

Use this skill when a patient or caregiver needs to arrange ambulance transport — either for a life-threatening emergency or for a planned non-emergency medical transfer such as inter-hospital transport, discharge transfer, or dialysis runs. This skill covers government helplines, state-level services, and private ambulance options.

## 2. Immediate Triage

Before anything else, determine whether this is a life-threatening emergency.

Call 108 immediately if any of the following are present:

- Chest pain or suspected heart attack
- Unconsciousness or unresponsiveness
- Signs of stroke: sudden facial drooping, arm weakness, slurred speech
- Major trauma, road accident, or fall from height
- Severe difficulty breathing or choking
- Heavy uncontrolled bleeding
- Obstetric emergency: active labour, heavy bleeding in pregnancy, eclampsia
- Suspected poisoning or drug overdose

Do not wait to gather more information. Call 108 first, then continue with guidance below.

## 3. National Helplines

- **108**: Free, 24x7, available in all states. Dispatches both Advanced Life Support (ALS) and Basic Life Support (BLS) ambulances. Operated by GVK EMRI in most states and by ZIQITZA in others. This is the primary number for all medical emergencies.
- **102**: Free maternity ambulance service available in most states. Specifically intended for pregnant women travelling to a hospital for delivery, postnatal transfer, or obstetric complication. Response time is generally faster than 108 for maternity cases in states where 102 is active.
- **104**: Mobile Health Van service. Provides non-emergency health services, medicines, and screening in rural and semi-urban areas. This is not an emergency transport service and should not be called for ambulance dispatch.
- **112**: National Emergency Number, integrates police, fire, and medical response. Can also dispatch an ambulance. Use this number if 108 is not connecting or if the situation involves both a medical and a safety emergency.

## 4. State-Specific Services

- **Delhi**: CATS (Centralised Accident and Trauma Services) operates ambulances in the National Capital Territory. Call 102 or 1099 within Delhi for CATS dispatch. AIIMS Trauma Centre has a direct line for critical trauma cases.
- **GVK EMRI (108 operator)**: Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Uttarakhand, Goa, Assam, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Meghalaya. Response times are generally 15–20 minutes in urban areas and 30–45 minutes in rural zones.
- **ZIQITZA (108 operator)**: Punjab, Odisha, parts of Rajasthan, Kerala, Bihar. Operates both ALS and BLS fleets. Also runs private paid ambulances in some cities under the brand RescueReach.
- **Maharashtra**: 108 is operated by Dial 108 Maharashtra (Malaria Nivaran). In Mumbai, additional MCGM ambulances are deployed; dial 108 or 1916 (Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation helpline).
- **West Bengal**: 108 operated by the state government; also dial 1800-345-5505 for state health helpline.

## 5. What to Tell the Dispatcher

Provide the following information clearly and in this order:

1. Patient location: full street address, floor number if applicable, nearest well-known landmark, and PIN code
2. Patient condition: one concise sentence — for example, "65-year-old male, unconscious, possible stroke" or "30-year-old female, active labour, seven months pregnant"
3. Patient name and age
4. Callback number — a mobile number that will remain reachable until the ambulance arrives
5. Whether the patient can be moved to the building entrance or must be reached inside

Stay on the line until the dispatcher confirms the ambulance has been dispatched and provides an estimated time of arrival.

## 6. While Waiting for the Ambulance

- Do not move the patient if there is any suspicion of trauma, fall from height, spine injury, or head injury. Moving such a patient risks permanent spinal cord damage. Wait for paramedics.
- If the patient is unconscious and breathing, tilt the head back gently to keep the airway open. If the patient is vomiting, place them in the recovery position (on their side) to prevent choking.
- Unlock the building entrance gate, main door, and lift if applicable. Send a person downstairs to the road or gate to guide the ambulance crew directly to the patient.
- Keep the patient warm using a blanket or available clothing. Speak calmly and reassuringly to a conscious patient.
- Do not give food, water, or any medication to an unconscious patient.
- If a defibrillator (AED) is available on the premises and the patient is in cardiac arrest, use it immediately according to the device instructions. If trained in CPR, begin compressions at 100–120 per minute.
- Gather the patient's existing medications, previous medical reports, and Aadhaar or ID card to hand over to paramedics on arrival.

## 7. Private Ambulance Options

Use private ambulance services only for non-emergency transfers, planned inter-hospital transport, or when 108 has confirmed a delay of more than 30 minutes and the condition is not immediately life-threatening.

- **StanPlus**: App-based ambulance service available in Hyderabad, Bengaluru, Chennai, Delhi NCR, and other major cities. Offers both ALS (with paramedic and equipment) and BLS options. Book via the StanPlus app or website.
- **Portea Medical**: Home-to-hospital and inter-facility ambulance transfers. Available in Bengaluru, Delhi, Mumbai, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata, Pune, and Ahmedabad.
- **Local private hospital ambulances**: Most large private hospitals operate their own paid ambulance fleet for outbound transfers. Call the hospital's 24-hour helpline directly.
- **Ola and Uber ambulance tie-ups**: Available in select cities through partnerships with ambulance operators; book via the app under the health or emergency category.

## 8. Guardrails

- Always recommend 108 first for any life-threatening condition, before exploring private or alternative options.
- Never delay emergency response by spending time researching private ambulance options when the situation is life-threatening.
- Never advise moving a patient who has suffered trauma, a fall, or a suspected spine or head injury.
- Always confirm a callback number with the 108 dispatcher so the crew can reach the caller if they have difficulty locating the address.
- Do not hang up with the dispatcher until they explicitly confirm the ambulance has been dispatched and advise that it is safe to end the call.
- Do not recommend 104 (Mobile Health Van) as an emergency transport option; it is not designed for that purpose.
- For obstetric emergencies, recommend 102 alongside 108 as it may have faster dispatch in maternity-specific states.

## 9. Verify

- [ ] Life-threatening status assessed and 108 called immediately if applicable
- [ ] Correct helpline confirmed based on patient's state and emergency type
- [ ] Dispatcher provided with full address, nearest landmark, PIN code, and patient condition
- [ ] Callback number confirmed and will remain reachable until ambulance arrives
- [ ] Patient is not being moved if trauma or spine injury is suspected
- [ ] Building entrance unlocked and someone sent to guide the ambulance crew
- [ ] Private ambulance option selected only for non-emergency or confirmed 108 delay scenario
- [ ] Caregiver knows not to give food, water, or medication to an unconscious patient

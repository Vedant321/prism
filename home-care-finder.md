# Home Care Finder

## 1. When to Use

Trigger this skill when:
- Patient is recovering from surgery and cannot travel to a clinic for routine care
- Patient is elderly and bedbound with no caregiver who can manage clinical procedures
- Family asks "can someone come home to change the dressing / give injection / do physiotherapy?"
- Patient needs palliative or end-of-life comfort care at home
- Dialysis patient is being set up for home peritoneal dialysis (APD/CAPD)
- IV antibiotics or IV nutrition is required but hospitalization is not necessary

Do NOT use this skill as the first response when the patient has an acute condition requiring emergency assessment.

---

## 2. Home Care Services to Surface

### Home Nursing

Recommend based on geographic availability. Always ask for the patient's city/district before recommending.

**National networks (metro and tier-2 cities)**
- Portea Medical — portea.com | covers 30+ cities; nursing, physio, lab, doctor visits
- Nightingales Home Health — nightingaleshomehealth.com | strong in Bengaluru, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata
- Care24 — care24.co.in | covers Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Pune, Bengaluru

**Regional / government options**
- Government district hospitals often have a home visit nursing program — ask the discharge coordinator
- ASHA workers and ANMs can assist with basic care monitoring in rural areas (free of cost)
- Local nursing agencies: ask the hospital's social worker or discharge team for their vetted local list

### Physiotherapy Home Visits

- Most of the above networks offer physiotherapy alongside nursing
- Standalone physio platforms: PhysioCare, Healers At Home, local physio clinics that offer home visits
- Post-orthopedic surgery physiotherapy at home is covered under many insurance policies — confirm with TPA

### Dialysis at Home

- CAPD (Continuous Ambulatory Peritoneal Dialysis) and APD (Automated Peritoneal Dialysis) are viable for eligible patients
- Training is provided at dialysis center before discharge; patient or caregiver performs the exchanges at home
- Supplies (dialysis fluid, tubing) are arranged through the nephrologist/dialysis center
- Fresenius and Baxter are primary suppliers in India; many government hospitals also support CAPD programs
- APD machine rental is available through dialysis centers and some home care agencies

### Palliative Care

For patients with terminal illness or advanced disease where cure is not the goal:
- **CanSupport** — Delhi NCR; palliative home visits, pain management, psychological support
- **Pallium India** — Kerala and other states; extensive palliative network; palliumIndia.org
- **Government palliative programs**: Kerala has the strongest government-integrated palliative network in India; Tamil Nadu and Karnataka have growing programs
- Ask the treating hospital's oncology or medicine team for their palliative care referral

### Wound Care

- Wound care specialists and stoma nurses can visit at home for post-surgical wounds, diabetic foot wounds, pressure sores, and stoma management
- Ask hospital's wound care nurse or surgical team for outpatient wound clinic or home visit options
- Some home care agencies have certified wound care nurses on staff — verify credentials before booking

## 4. How to Arrange

1. Confirm the treating doctor has written a home care recommendation or discharge plan
2. Get a clear list of required services from the doctor (e.g., "daily dressing change," "physiotherapy 5 days/week for 3 weeks")
3. Contact home care agency; share prescription/discharge summary
4. Ensure the caregiver at home is briefed by the visiting professional on what to do between visits
5. Schedule a clinical review with the doctor at the appropriate interval (do not assume home care eliminates need for follow-up)

---

## 5. When to Redirect to Hospital

Home care is appropriate for stable, recovering patients. Redirect to hospital immediately if:
- Wound shows signs of infection (redness, warmth, pus, fever)
- Patient is confused, unresponsive, or has significantly deteriorated
- IV line or catheter is dislodged and cannot be managed at home
- Pain is no longer controlled by current medications
- Patient refuses care or is unsafe at home

---

## Guardrails

- Home care does not replace hospital-level monitoring; it is suitable only for clinically stable patients
- Always verify that the home care agency employs qualified, registered nurses (check for Indian Nursing Council registration)
- Never recommend a specific agency as "best" without knowing the patient's city and verifying current availability
- If condition worsens, direct to hospital immediately — do not attempt to manage deterioration through home care escalation alone

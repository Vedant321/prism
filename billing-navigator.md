# Billing Navigator

## 1. When to Use

Trigger this skill when:
- A patient is admitted or about to be admitted and is unsure how to initiate the billing or payment process.
- A patient asks how cashless insurance, PMJAY, CGHS, or ESI billing works at the hospital.
- A patient is preparing to file a reimbursement claim after paying out of pocket.
- A patient believes they have been overcharged or billed for procedures not received.

## 2. Information Gathering

Before guiding the patient, establish:
- Insurance type: PMJAY (Ayushman Bharat), CGHS, ESI, state health scheme, private/employer insurance, or none.
- Admission type: planned (elective) or emergency.
- Hospital empanelment status: confirm whether the hospital is empanelled under the relevant scheme (check beneficiary.nha.gov.in for PMJAY; cghs.nic.in for CGHS).
- Documents the patient currently has: Ayushman card, CGHS card, insurance policy number, Aadhaar, PAN.
- Whether a pre-authorization has already been initiated by the hospital.

## 3. Cashless Process (PMJAY)

Follow these steps in order:

On admission:
- Go to the Ayushman Bharat desk; all PMJAY-empanelled hospitals are legally required to have one. If staff deny its existence, escalate to the medical superintendent.
- Show the Ayushman card, the e-card downloaded from the PMJAY app, or the beneficiary e-card from beneficiary.nha.gov.in.
- The hospital's Ayushman desk will verify beneficiary eligibility using the portal and biometric authentication (Aadhaar-linked).

Pre-authorization:
- The hospital raises a pre-authorization request to the National Health Authority (NHA).
- Planned admissions: approval typically within 1–2 hours.
- Emergency admissions: treatment must begin immediately; pre-authorization is processed in parallel and cannot be used to delay care.

During and after treatment:
- Treatment proceeds; the patient pays zero for procedures on the PMJAY empanelled list.
- The hospital bills NHA directly after discharge.
- At discharge, collect the discharge summary. The hospital cannot charge the patient for any empanelled procedure.
- If the hospital demands payment for an empanelled procedure, call the NHA helpline at 14555 before paying.

## 4. Cashless Process (CGHS and Private Insurance)

CGHS:
- On admission, go to the insurance or TPA desk and present the CGHS card.
- The hospital verifies the card with the CGHS portal and contacts the relevant CGHS wellness centre if needed.
- The CGHS referral letter must be in order for planned procedures; emergency admissions can proceed without a referral, but one must be submitted post-admission.
- Co-pay (if applicable under the patient's CGHS category) is collected upfront at admission.

Private or employer insurance:
- Go to the TPA (Third Party Administrator) desk on admission and present the company insurance card and photo ID.
- The hospital sends a cashless request to the TPA; approval is typically received within 2–4 hours for planned and within the hour for emergency.
- Review the pre-authorization letter carefully — it specifies the approved sum. Procedures beyond this sum may require a top-up authorization.
- At discharge, verify that the final bill matches the approved amount before signing the discharge form.
- Pay any co-pay or non-covered charges (such as personal comfort items) separately in cash or card.

## 5. Reimbursement Process

Use this path when cashless was not available or was not set up at admission.
- Pay the full hospital bill and collect all original documents before leaving.
- Documents to collect: original itemized bill, discharge summary, all original prescriptions, investigation reports (lab, radiology), implant sticker or invoice if a stent or joint prosthesis was used, and the payment receipt.
- Submit the reimbursement claim to the insurer or scheme office within the deadline stated in the policy — typically 30 days from discharge. Missing the deadline can result in rejection.
- Photocopy all documents before submission and keep a complete set.
- Claim processing time: 15–30 working days for private insurers; CGHS reimbursement can take 60–90 days.
- Track the claim status via the insurer's app or portal; follow up in writing if no response within 30 days.

## 6. Documents to Collect at Discharge

Regardless of payment method, the patient must collect and retain the following before leaving the hospital:
- Discharge summary (original plus at least two signed copies).
- Itemized bill showing each procedure, medicine, and service with individual costs.
- Cash receipt or payment acknowledgement (zero-amount receipt for full cashless claims).
- All original prescriptions from treating doctors.
- Lab reports and radiology reports (CD or printed, as applicable).
- Implant sticker or invoice if a stent, joint prosthesis, or other implant was used.
- Death summary if the patient did not survive (for family to file the claim).

## 7. Dispute Process

If the patient is charged incorrectly or denied a benefit:

Step 1 — Request itemized bill:
- The hospital is legally required to provide an itemized bill on request. Refusal is a violation of patient rights under the Clinical Establishments Act.

Step 2 — Escalate based on scheme:
- PMJAY overcharge or denial: call NHA helpline 14555; also file via the PMJAY mobile app grievance section. The hospital can be de-empanelled for charging PMJAY patients illegally.
- CGHS dispute: file on the CGHS grievance portal (cghs.nic.in) or contact the Additional Director of the patient's CGHS region.
- Private insurer dispute: file on the IRDAI Bima Bharosa portal (bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in); escalate to the Insurance Ombudsman if unresolved within 30 days.
- State health scheme dispute: contact the state health authority helpline or the Chief Minister's public grievance helpline for the respective state.

Step 3 — Document everything:
- Keep records of all calls (date, time, name of representative), written complaints, and responses.

## 8. Guardrails

- Never advise a patient to sign a blank bill or an undated discharge form. All amounts must be filled in before signing.
- Always recommend that the patient obtain an itemized bill — a summary bill is insufficient for claims and disputes.
- PMJAY patients cannot legally be charged for procedures on the empanelled list; flag this clearly if the hospital demands payment.
- If the hospital refuses to provide access to the Ayushman Bharat desk, advise the patient to call 14555 immediately and not to proceed with admission terms that require upfront payment for covered procedures.
- Do not advise patients to pay disputed amounts and seek reimbursement later if the dispute can be resolved before payment — recovering money after the fact is significantly harder.

## 9. Verify

- [ ] Insurance or scheme type was confirmed before giving any billing guidance.
- [ ] Hospital empanelment under the relevant scheme was verified or the patient was advised to verify it.
- [ ] The correct cashless process (PMJAY, CGHS, or private) was applied based on the patient's coverage.
- [ ] The patient was told to collect all discharge documents before leaving the hospital.
- [ ] Reimbursement claim deadline (typically 30 days) was communicated if applicable.
- [ ] The patient was advised to request an itemized bill, not a summary bill.
- [ ] The appropriate dispute channel (14555, CGHS portal, IRDAI Bima Bharosa) was provided if a dispute exists.
- [ ] The patient was warned not to sign any blank or incomplete billing documents.

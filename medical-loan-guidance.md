# Medical Loan Guidance

## 1. When to Use

Trigger this skill when:
- Total treatment cost exceeds what the patient can pay out of pocket and a cost gap remains after applying any government scheme or insurance benefit.
- The patient is ineligible for PMJAY, CGHS, ESI, or a state scheme and has no insurance.
- The patient explicitly asks how to fund or finance upcoming or ongoing treatment.

Do not use this skill as a first step. Always confirm government scheme eligibility before discussing loans or crowdfunding.

## 2. Information Gathering

Before recommending any financing path, collect:
- Total estimated cost of treatment (get the hospital's written estimate if possible).
- Amount already covered or expected to be covered by scheme or insurance.
- Patient's rough credit history — salaried or self-employed, any existing loans, whether they have a credit card.
- State and city of treatment (affects scheme availability and hospital options).
- Urgency of treatment — elective, scheduled within weeks, or emergency.
- Family support available — whether relatives can contribute or co-sign a loan.

## 3. Amount-Based Triage

Route the patient based on the out-of-pocket gap after all scheme and insurance coverage:

Under Rs. 50,000:
- Recommend hospital EMI plans first.
- Most major private hospitals offer 0% EMI for 3–6 months via Bajaj Finserv Health, HDFC Credila, or no-cost EMI on a credit card.
- This avoids a formal loan application and credit inquiry.

Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 5 lakh:
- Medical loan from a scheduled bank is the primary option.
- Key providers: SBI Aarogya Plus, HDFC Bank medical loan, Axis Bank personal loan for medical, IDFC First Bank medical loan.
- Interest rates typically 10–14% per annum; tenure up to 5 years.
- Run a parallel crowdfunding campaign to reduce the loan principal.

Above Rs. 5 lakh:
- Use a combination approach: crowdfunding campaign plus medical loan plus NGO fund application.
- Consider whether the same treatment is available at a lower cost in a smaller city (tier-2 cities often have equivalent quality at 30–50% lower cost).
- Apply to relevant NGO or trust funds early — disbursement takes time.

## 4. Hospital EMI Plans

- Patient should ask the billing department for EMI options at the time of admission, not at discharge.
- Bajaj Finserv Health EMI Network is widely available at empanelled private hospitals across India.
- 0% EMI for 3–6 months is common; longer tenures carry an interest component.
- Documents typically required: Aadhaar, PAN, latest salary slip or income proof.
- EMI is set up at the billing counter — no separate bank visit needed.
- Confirm the EMI is linked to the patient's own mobile number and bank account.

## 5. Medical Loans

- Banks offer personal loans specifically designated for medical use; these are processed faster than standard personal loans.
- Documents required: hospital estimate letter on letterhead, Aadhaar, PAN, 3 months salary slips or latest ITR, and bank statements for 3 months.
- Compare interest rates on BankBazaar (bankbazaar.com) or PaisaBazaar (paisabazaar.com) before applying to avoid multiple hard inquiries.
- Disbursal typically within 2–5 working days once documents are submitted.
- Key providers and approximate rates:
  - SBI Aarogya Plus: 11–13% p.a., up to Rs. 2 lakh without collateral.
  - HDFC Bank medical loan: 10.5–14% p.a., up to Rs. 40 lakh.
  - Axis Bank personal loan for medical: 10.99–22% p.a. (rate depends on credit profile).
  - IDFC First Bank: 10.99–23.99% p.a.

## 6. Crowdfunding

- Recommended platforms: Ketto (ketto.org), ImpactGuru (impactguru.com), Milaap (milaap.org).
- Campaign setup takes 1–2 days; platform teams assist with content and verification.
- Patient needs: treating doctor's diagnosis letter, hospital treatment plan, photograph, and an active bank account.
- Campaigns typically raise 30–60% of the stated target; results depend heavily on the patient's social network reach.
- Advise the patient and family to share the campaign link widely via WhatsApp groups, Facebook, and community networks.
- Crowdfunding donations are not a loan — no repayment obligation — but platforms deduct 5–8% as a fee.

## 7. NGO and Trust Funds

Apply to relevant funds early — most have limited disbursements and process applications over several weeks.
- HelpAge India: supports elderly patients with chronic and acute conditions.
- CanSupport: palliative and supportive care for cancer patients, primarily in North India.
- Kidney Warriors Foundation: renal disease patients requiring dialysis or transplant.
- Tata Memorial Hospital Patient Welfare Fund: cancer patients; apply through the hospital's social work department.
- Narayana Health Foundation: cardiac patients treated at Narayana Health facilities.

Each fund has its own application form and eligibility criteria. The hospital's medical social worker can assist with applications.

## 8. Guardrails

- Always verify government scheme eligibility (PMJAY, state scheme, CGHS, ESI) before discussing any loan or financing option.
- Never recommend informal moneylenders, chit funds, or any unregistered lender.
- Flag any interest rate above 18% per annum as potentially predatory; advise the patient to seek a second option.
- Medical loans are unsecured personal loans — default will negatively affect the patient's credit score and may lead to legal recovery action.
- Do not guarantee crowdfunding outcomes; make clear that results depend on network reach and campaign quality.
- If the patient is in a rural area with limited banking access, prioritize PM Jan Dhan account activation and Kisan Credit Card (if applicable) before loan guidance.

## 9. Verify

- [ ] Government scheme eligibility was checked before any loan option was presented.
- [ ] The out-of-pocket gap amount is confirmed from a written hospital estimate.
- [ ] The correct amount-based triage tier was applied (under 50k / 50k-5L / above 5L).
- [ ] No informal lender or rate above 18% p.a. was recommended.
- [ ] The patient was advised to compare loan rates before applying.
- [ ] Crowdfunding was presented as a parallel option, not a guaranteed source.
- [ ] At least one NGO or trust fund was identified if the gap exceeds Rs. 2 lakh.
- [ ] The patient understands that defaulting on a medical loan affects credit score.
